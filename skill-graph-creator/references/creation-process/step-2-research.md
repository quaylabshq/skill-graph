# Step 2: Research

**Status: MANDATORY — never skip this step.**

Before implementation, research how similar systems are structured, what best practices exist, and what standards apply to the domain.

## Why Research Is Non-Negotiable

Building a skill graph without research means relying solely on existing knowledge, which may be incomplete or outdated. Research ensures the skill graph reflects current best practices and avoids known pitfalls.

## What to Research

1. **Similar systems** — How are existing tools/frameworks in this domain structured?
2. **Best practices** — What does the community recommend for this type of work?
3. **Standards** — Are there industry standards, conventions, or specifications that apply?
4. **Common patterns** — What patterns have been proven effective in this domain?
5. **Known pitfalls** — What mistakes are commonly made? What should be avoided?

## How to Research

Apply the [research framework](../research-framework/overview.md):

1. Use web search to find documentation, tutorials, and community discussions
2. Examine official documentation for the relevant tools and technologies
3. Look for reference implementations and examples
4. Identify common architectural patterns
5. Synthesize findings into actionable requirements

## Output

Research should produce:

- A clear understanding of the domain's best practices
- Identified standards or conventions that the skill graph should follow
- A list of patterns that should inform the graph structure
- Any discovered constraints or requirements that weren't captured in step 1

## Rules

1. Research is performed BEFORE planning the graph structure
2. Findings may trigger return to step 1 for additional clarification
3. Document key findings — they inform decomposition decisions in step 4
4. Do not over-research — focus on information that directly impacts the skill graph design
